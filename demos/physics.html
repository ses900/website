Physics Fundamentals

1. Force

According to Newton's Second Law, force is the product of mass and acceleration:

F = m × a

where:
F = force (in newtons, N)
m = mass (in kilograms, kg)
a = acceleration (in meters per second squared, m/s²)

2. Work

Work is defined as the force applied over a distance in the direction of the force:

W = F × d

where:
W = work (in joules, J)
F = force (in newtons, N)
d = displacement (in meters, m)

3. Power

Power is the rate at which work is done over time:

P = W / t

where:
P = power (in watts, W)
W = work (in joules, J)
t = time (in seconds, s)
